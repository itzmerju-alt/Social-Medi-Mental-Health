Introduction: Exploring the Impact of Social Media on Mental Health

In my final project, I have decided to visualize the intricate relationship between social media usage and mental health, seeking to unravel the various factors that contribute to the well-being of individuals in the digital age. I have used two datasets that were available in Kaggle as https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/souvikahmed071/social-media-and-mental-health and https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/ngugimacharia/mental-health-and-social-media and made necessary adjustments so that it can be easily accessible and read into R in R studio. These datasets was originally collected for a data science and machine learning project that aimed at investigating the potential correlation between the amount of time an individual spends on social media and the impact it has on their mental health which was exactly what I wanted to visualize here. These datasets under examination encapsulates a comprehensive set of variables, ranging from demographic details such as age, gender, and occupation status, to nuanced aspects like affiliation with organizations, self-reported mental health status, and usage patterns. Exploring these dimensions allows us to discern patterns and trends that may shed light on the complex interplay between online activities and mental well-being.
