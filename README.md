Analyzed the relationship between social media usage and mental health outcomes using two Kaggle datasets with 480+ survey respondents.
Key Findings
270 out of 481 respondents showed high anxiety scores correlated with heavy social media use. University students had the highest mental health impact scores. Facebook had the highest purposeless usage, followed by Instagram. Females reported significantly higher mental health impacts than males, and single individuals who interacted very often on social media most frequently reported poor mental health.
Tools & Methods
Built in R using tidyverse, ggplot2, and readxl. Methods included data cleaning, feature engineering, composite metric construction, and data visualization.
